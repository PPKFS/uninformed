Divisibility relates a number (called N) to a number (called M)
when the remainder after dividing M by N is 0.

The verb to divide (it divides, they divide, it divided, it is divisible)
implies the divisibility relation. The verb to be a factor of implies the
divisibility relation.

When play begins:
	if 2 is a factor of 8, say "2 is a factor of 8.";
	if 2 is not a factor of 17, say "2 is not a factor of 17.";
	if 3 divides 6, say "3 divides 6.";
	if 3 divides 17, say "3 divides 17."
